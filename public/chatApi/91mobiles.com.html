Reasonably priced Android smartphone

Display and Build
 
The Samsung Galaxy Core Prime flaunts a 4.5-inch LCD multi-touch display with a resolution of 480 x 800 pixels translating to a pixel density of 218 ppi. The so called PLS or Plane to Line Switching panel makes sure that the user can enjoy greater amount of brightness and spectacular viewing even from wide off axis angles.
 
The upcoming Samsung Prime has been wrapped in an 8.8mm thick shell which is available in black and white finishes.
 
Processor and OS
 
Sandwiched between the front and rear panels of the smartphone there is a quad-core processor clocked at 1.3GHz and working in combination with a 1GB RAM. It runs on the latest Android v4.4.2 OS, popularly known as KitKat thereby offering the latest perks and tools from Google.
 
Camera and Storage
 
At the rear panel of the Samsung Galaxy Core Prime, you can find a 5MP shooter which has a resolution of 2,592 x 1,944 pixels and comes with support for features like face detection, geo-tagging, LED flash and autofocus. The rear camera can shoot full HD videos at 30fps as well. For video calls and selfie obsessed people, the phone has a 2MP snapper on the front.
 
The smartphone comes with an onboard storage capacity of 8GB which can be expanded up to 32GB via microSD slot.
 
Battery and Connectivity
 
To ensure an interruption free service for a decent amount of time, the Samsung Galaxy Core Prime is armed with a 2,000mAh Li-ion battery. Moving to the connectivity and networking section, this dual SIM smartphone supports features like GPS with AGPS, USB port, 3G, DLNA, Wi-Fi Direct, Mobile hotspot and Bluetooth.

Verdict

The Samsung Galaxy Core Prime is an affordably priced smartphone which has been targeted at people who want to explore the latest Android tools and apps and prefer decent performance as well. The combined presence of powerful features in a reasonably priced Samsung smartphone is quite appreciable.
